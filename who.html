Board of Directors

YC

Yen Chen

Founder of Space Zero

Yen is the President and Executive Director at Space Zero. She has a particular gift and spiritual instinct for bringing ideas from zero to life. Over the past decade she has founded and led initiatives across San Francisco, New York, and China, creating spaces that support spiritual growth for entrepreneurs and innovators. Adam Goldstein

Chairman

Adam is the Chairman of the board at Space Zero. He co-founded the travel site Hipmunk (acquired by SAP Concur in 2016) after studying electrical and mechanical engineering at MIT. He went on to become a Visiting Scientist at Michael Levin's lab at Tufts, then a graduate student in Physiology, Anatomy, and Genetics at Oxford. At Space Zero, he brings entrepreneurial clarity and depth of wisdom to the question of how nature's own logic of emergence might shape the institutions we build next.

David Bachman

Board Member

David is Senior Executive at NVIDIA. He earned a Ph.D. in Applied Physics from Caltech with a focus on plasma physics and fusion. After four years at Lockheed Martin as a Systems Engineer, he joined NVIDIA, where he has held engineering and management roles for nearly twenty-five years and contributed to the company's emergence as a foundational platform for artificial intelligence. Shinzen Young

Founder · Unified Mindfulness

Shinzen is an American mindfulness teacher and neuroscience research consultant whose systematic approach, Unified Mindfulness, has led to collaborations with Harvard Medical School, Carnegie Mellon, and the University of Vermont. He currently co-directs the Science Enhanced Mindful Awareness Lab at the University of Arizona. After studying Asian languages at UCLA and Buddhist studies at Wisconsin, he lived for three years as a Shingon monk at Mount Koya in Japan. He is the author of The Science of Enlightenment. At Space Zero, his lifelong study of where practice meets science anchors the conviction that contemplative depth is something a place can be built to support.

Scientific Advisors

SL

Sara Lazar

Associate Professor · Harvard Medical School · Mass General

Sara is an Associate Professor in Psychiatry at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School. Her lab uses MRI to investigate the neuroplastic changes that follow mindfulness and yoga practice, and how those changes correlate with reductions in stress, anxiety, and chronic pain. She is among the world's leading researchers on the neuroscience of meditation, and her recent work explores the intersection of meditation, adult development, and virtue. At Space Zero, her work meets Lab Zero's central enquiry: that what unfolds in the body during sustained practice is not metaphor, but measurable change in the substrate of a life.

AS

Adam Safron

Independent Researcher · Allen Discovery Center, Tufts

Adam is an independent researcher working at the intersection of consciousness, agency, psychedelics, artificial life, and AI alignment. He is the author of Integrated World Modeling Theory, which uses the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference framework to combine insights from Integrated Information Theory and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory. He currently collaborates with Michael Levin at the Allen Discovery Center at Tufts, and has held appointments at Johns Hopkins, Indiana University, and the Institute for Advanced Consciousness Studies. KD

Kati Devaney

Contemplative Neuroscientist · UC Berkeley

Kati is a contemplative neuroscientist and meditation practitioner with more than twenty years of practice. She earned her Ph.D. using fMRI to study attention and prediction updating in experienced Vipassana meditators, and completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Harvard Medical School. She is currently a researcher at the UC Berkeley Center for the Science of Psychedelics, co-founder of The Berkeley Alembic, and Chief Science Officer of Jhourney. At Space Zero, she embodies the figure Lab Zero is built around: a researcher whose science is rooted in decades of practice, where the laboratory and the cushion are the same enquiry.

ML

Michael Levin

Director · Allen Discovery Center, Tufts University

Michael is the Vannevar Bush Distinguished Professor of Biology at Tufts, Director of the Allen Discovery Center, and Associate Faculty at Harvard's Wyss Institute. His group works at the interface of developmental biology, cognitive science, and computer science, studying how bioelectric signals coordinate cells into the collective intelligence that builds and regenerates anatomy. He is known for the co-discovery of bioelectric control of growth and form, the creation of xenobots, and a body of work that has reframed how we think about cognition across scales of life. At Space Zero, his work meets the lab's most fundamental question (what does the redwood already know?) and reframes intelligence itself as something the living world has been practising for a very long time.

Paul is an AI safety researcher whose work focuses on AI control evaluations, interpretability-based goal detection, and formal approaches to secure alignment.
