Two practices, one coastline

Space Zero is the somatic sibling of HUM Lab. Adjacent, not subsidiary — different work, one shared coastline, one shared concern for living well with attention.

Adjacency

Space Zero & HUM Lab

Space Zero

Somatic & embodied

Meditation, breath, shared creativity, movement, silence. Visiting speakers, group activities, and regular Emergent events where the agenda is set by whoever arrives.

The work of remembering that the body is where attention actually lives.

A not-for-profit 501(c)(3), volunteer-led, open to change by the people who make it.

HUM Lab · adjacent

Cognitive & technical

AI alignment research, ecological awareness, and a solutions-focussed orientation toward the world's present problems. Theory, tools, collaborations.

The work of building technology that serves the long now.

HUM Lab operates as a distinct organisation on the same coastline; we collaborate often, but each holds its own character.

We believe the technical questions and the somatic questions are the same question, asked from different directions. The work of alignment — human, technical, ecological — is continuous with the work of learning to inhabit a body.

How we hold the work

Commitments

— 01

Open to change

The space is shaped by whoever shows up. We expect to be different next year than we are this year, and we treat that as healthy.

— 02

Care before capability

We prefer a small thing done with care over an impressive thing done hastily. We hold that preference across meditation, creativity, and technology alike.

— 03

All bodies welcome

Backgrounds, abilities, beliefs, levels of familiarity. Quietness is a welcome. Showing up matters more than knowing what to do.

We are the people who happen to be in this room, on this coast, for this particular stretch of years. That is enough.
— Space Zero, 2026
