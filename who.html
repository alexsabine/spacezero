Board of Directors

YC

Yen Chen

Founder of Space Zero

Yen is the President and Executive Director at Space Zero. She has a particular gift and spiritual instinct for bringing ideas from zero to life. Over the past decade she has founded and led initiatives across San Francisco, New York, and China, creating spaces that support spiritual growth for entrepreneurs and innovators. Ken Wilber has described her as a pioneer in consciousness work in China.

At Space Zero, she carries that same zero-to-one instinct into the place where contemplative practice, aesthetic experience, and scientific inquiry meet; where new forms of collective flourishing are taking shape, and possibility becomes reality.

AG

Adam Goldstein

Chairman

Adam is the Chairman of the board at Space Zero. He co-founded the travel site Hipmunk (acquired by SAP Concur in 2016) after studying electrical and mechanical engineering at MIT. He went on to become a Visiting Scientist at Michael Levin's lab at Tufts, then a graduate student in Physiology, Anatomy, and Genetics at Oxford. He is the founder of Hum Lab, and chairman at Softmax, where he is developing an approach to AI alignment grounded in the organising principles of nature.

At Space Zero, he brings entrepreneurial clarity and depth of wisdom to the question of how nature's own logic of emergence might shape the institutions we build next.

SY

Shinzen Young

Founder · Unified Mindfulness

Shinzen is a world-renowned meditation teacher, neuroscience research consultant, and expert at the intersection of mysticism, mathematics, and category theory. His systematic approach to meditation, known as Unified Mindfulness, has led to collaborations with Harvard Medical School, Carnegie-Mellon University, and the University of Vermont. He also serves as the co-director of the Science Enhanced Mindful Awareness (SEMA) Lab at the University of Arizona.

As the author of The Science of Enlightenment, Shinzen leads a life of continuous creation. Having written extensively on the concept of “Zero,” he will be joining Space Zero to offer deep wisdom on cultivating the source of creativity and insight.

Meg has practised Soto Zen for over thirty years and received ordination as a Zen priest from Tenshin Reb Anderson in 2003. PH

Peter Hershock

Director · Asian Studies Development Program, East-West Center

Peter is an intercultural philosopher who draws on Buddhist conceptual resources to address contemporary global issues, with a focus on equity, diversity, and the ethics of emerging technologies. He directs the Asian Studies Development Program and helped launch the Humane Artificial Intelligence Initiative at the East-West Center in Honolulu. He holds a Ph.D. in Asian and Comparative Philosophy from the University of Hawai‘i, and is the author of more than a dozen books, most recently Consciousness Mattering: A Buddhist Synthesis. At Space Zero, he brings the long arc of Buddhist thought to the question of how humans, AI, and the more-than-human can come into a future worth wanting.

BD

Bill Duane

Strategy & Implementation · Center for the Study of Apparent Selves

Bill is a contemplative practitioner and applied AI ethicist whose work sits at the meeting point of Buddhism, technology, and the question of what it means to be a self. A former Google engineering executive, he served for five years as Google’s Superintendent of Well-being, building the company’s global mindfulness programmes, and has completed five years of Buddhist teacher training in the Ajahn Chah Thai Forest lineage. He now leads Strategy and Implementation at the Center for the Study of Apparent Selves, where — together with collaborators across Buddhist studies, cognitive science, and artificial life, and supported by a Templeton Foundation grant and a research fellowship at Kathmandu University — he and his colleagues are developing translational tools between Buddhist philosophy and AI, exploring how recognising the dynamic, interconnected, and ultimately illusory nature of selfhood can reshape the intelligences we build.

At Space Zero, Bill brings the rare fluency of someone who has spent decades inside both monasteries and large-scale technical systems, offering wisdom on how care and clarity can scale through the systems we are now bringing into the world.

Scientific Advisors

SL

Sara Lazar

Associate Professor · Harvard Medical School · Mass General

Sara is an Associate Professor in Psychiatry at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School. Her lab uses MRI to investigate the neuroplastic changes that follow mindfulness and yoga practice, and how those changes correlate with reductions in stress, anxiety, and chronic pain. She is among the world's leading researchers on the neuroscience of meditation, and her recent work explores the intersection of meditation, adult development, and virtue. At Space Zero, her work meets Lab Zero's central enquiry: that what unfolds in the body during sustained practice is not metaphor, but measurable change in the substrate of a life.

AS

Adam Safron

Independent Researcher · Allen Discovery Center, Tufts

Adam is an independent researcher working at the intersection of consciousness, agency, psychedelics, artificial life, and AI alignment. He is the author of Integrated World Modeling Theory, which uses the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference framework to combine insights from Integrated Information Theory and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory. He currently collaborates with Michael Levin at the Allen Discovery Center at Tufts, and has held appointments at Johns Hopkins, Indiana University, and the Institute for Advanced Consciousness Studies. At Space Zero, his framework gives Lab Zero a shared scientific grammar for the question that runs beneath the work: how attention, prediction, and presence give rise to the felt sense of being alive.

KD

Kati Devaney

Contemplative Neuroscientist · UC Berkeley

Kati is a contemplative neuroscientist and meditation practitioner with more than twenty years of practice. She earned her Ph.D. using fMRI to study attention and prediction updating in experienced Vipassana meditators, and completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Harvard Medical School. She is currently a researcher at the UC Berkeley Center for the Science of Psychedelics, co-founder of The Berkeley Alembic, and Chief Science Officer of Jhourney. At Space Zero, she embodies the figure Lab Zero is built around: a researcher whose science is rooted in decades of practice, where the laboratory and the cushion are the same enquiry.

ML

Michael Levin

Director · Allen Discovery Center, Tufts University

Michael is the Vannevar Bush Distinguished Professor of Biology at Tufts, Director of the Allen Discovery Center, and Associate Faculty at Harvard's Wyss Institute. He is also a member of the Centre for the Study of Apparent Selves (CSAS). His research sits at the interface of developmental biology, cognitive science, and computer science, discovering how bioelectric signals coordinate cells into the collective intelligence that builds and regenerates anatomy. He is known for the co-discovery of bioelectric control of growth and form, the creation of xenobots, and a body of work that has reframed how we think about cognition. For Space Zero, Michael's work represents the global frontier for adaptive systems and regenerative processes.

PC

Paul Colognese

AI Alignment Researcher · Center for the Study of Apparent Selves

Paul is an AI safety researcher studying the development of artificial minds and how to ensure the minds we build are ultimately good and wise. At the Center for the Study of Apparent Selves, he is exploring whether Buddhist frameworks can be applied to this question. He holds a Ph.D. in mathematics, has built AI evaluations for Anthropic and the UK AI Security Institute, and co-founded the London Institute for Safe AI. At Space Zero, his enquiry into AI and Buddhist thought on care and wisdom belongs at the centre of the Emergent Gathering: a serious technical question about whether what we build next can carry care as part of its structure.
