What happens here
Practice

Space Zero is a place of gathered attention. The things we do here are simple — sitting together, breathing, making, listening, noticing — and the simplicity is the point.

All practices are open to any level of familiarity, and most are offered without cost. Donations support the space and the practitioners.

Offerings

What we do

— 01

Meditation

Silent sits, guided practice, and contemplative walks along the coast. We hold space for the kind of attention that notices itself noticing — without agenda, without performance.

Typical rhythm Mornings, 7:00–7:45. Longer monthly sits. Occasional silent retreat days.

— 02

Shared creativity

Writing, drawing, sound, and making together. No performance, no judgement, no finished product required. The practice is the room and the hours and the company of other people attending to their materials.

Typical rhythm Evenings, 7:00–9:00. Weekly. Occasional all-day making sessions.

— 03

Visiting speakers

Practitioners, researchers, and craftspeople whose work meets the body, the ecology, or the present question. Conversations more than lectures — we value thinking out loud, together.

Typical rhythm Monthly or as visitors pass through. Announced via the newsletter.

— 04

Emergent events

Gatherings with no fixed agenda. The room decides what it needs. Held with care by the people in attendance — facilitated but not led, shaped in the moment, remembered afterward.

Typical rhythm Quarterly, or whenever a seed is planted. Bring what you arrive with.
