Practice

Space Zero is a physical and relational space designed to make outer impact through cultivating embodied relationship with self, others, and the living system. What we do

Lab Zero Science, Nature & Technology

At the edge of the Pacific, where the known meets the unknowable, we turn scientific attention toward the questions that matter at the root level. What is the source of love? How does creativity arise in a body, a mind, a community? How does interdependence move through all living things?

Lab Zero gathers biologists, mathematicians, neuroscientists, physicists, and consciousness researchers to inquire across the boundaries that usually keep disciplines apart, and across the boundaries between ways of knowing.

We study what the redwood already knows. What starlings practice. What iceplant along the Battery Point cliffs makes effortless. Nature has always been the longer experiment; we are learning to read it.

The Emergent Gathering Humans, AI & More-than-Human

Something new is arriving; we do not yet know its shape. Large language models. Collective intelligences. Ecosystems held in our care, or thrown off balance by it. None of these is the thing it was a decade ago, and the next decade will not resemble this one.

The Emergent Gathering brings together technologists, philosophers, ecologists, and practitioners of attention to ask: what does benevolent emergence look like? How do we build with AI rather than against what it flattens? What would a future genuinely good for human and more-than-human life require of us, now?

We meet without an agenda and let the conversation find its form. Sometimes that means a lecture, sometimes a walk, sometimes silence. The question is the discipline.

Wisdom in Residence Spirit, Art & Philosophy

Knowledge written down stays on the page; wisdom happens between people and places. Space Zero holds residencies for spiritual teachers, artists, and philosophers who live here for a time: cooking, walking, working, teaching when asked, staying quiet when that is what the moment wants.

We believe in embodied transmission. A zen teacher sitting every morning in the same room changes the room. A poet writing for two months on the coast leaves something in the fog. These are not metaphors; this is how cultures have always cultivated the subtle.

Past residents arrive from contemplative lineages, from studios, from universities. What they share is a willingness to be present to ordinary life, to let wisdom take root in a place rather than only in pages.

Ecosystem Collaboration Collective Flourishing

No practice flourishes alone. Space Zero is local to Crescent City; it is also woven into a wider fabric of researchers, teachers, communities, and ecologies that stretch from the redwoods to the Pacific Rim and beyond.

We share what we learn. We host gatherings with partners further afield. We study how groups move together well: attunement, entrainment, the quiet conditions under which collaboration becomes effortless and care becomes structural rather than heroic.

Collective flourishing is not an aggregate of individual flourishing. It is a property of the between. That is the work here.

The Space A contemplative venue on the Crescent City coast

The Space is the physical heart of everything else on this page. A real building, on real ground, on the Crescent City coast of northern California, between the Pacific and the redwoods.

It is where people sit in the mornings. Where teachers give talks in the evenings. Where meals are shared, where the library lives, where the gardens are tended. The four practices above are all offered here, and each returns to this room at the end of the day.

A daily embodied practice is the ground everything else on this page stands on; and zero, in the mathematics we care about, is not nothing. It is the point from which new things become possible.
